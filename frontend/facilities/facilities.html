Facilities

everyone is welcome

Visitors with disabilities

Honey is dedicated to ensuring the festival is accessible for visitors with disabilities, with significant efforts made to accommodate their needs:

  • If you have a valid blue parking card, you will find parking spaces near the Main Entrance
  • At the Main Entrance, there is a dedicated elevator for visitors with disabilities, allow two companions (with a registered Honey ticket) to accompany them.
  • Guidance dogs are welcome on the festival grounds, as long as they're clearly identifiable.
  • Adapted toilets are available throughout the festival site.
  • The festival grounds feature wooden floors and concrete paths to ensure smooth accesability. To access the Mainstage or The Nectar Lounge, simply speak to one of the stewards or volunteers, who will assist with escorting you.

If you have any additional questions regarding accessibility, feel free to contact honey@honey.com

the center of the hive

Main Stage

The heart of the festival, where energy electrifies the air and crowds gather to experience unforgettable performances. With a stunning backdrop of visuals and lights, this is where the best of live music, art, and culture collide, creating a space for everyone to dance, sing, and lose themselves in the rhythm of the moment.

where the bees sleep

Camping Zone

A cozy, laid-back space where Honeybees can set up camp and unwind under the stars. With designated areas for tents, chill-out zones, and communal fire pits, it’s the perfect spot to relax, connect with fellow Honeybees, and share stories from the day’s adventures.

where the bees chill

The Nectar Lounge

A beautifully decorated tent with hammocks, cushions, and soft lighting where Honeybees can rest, meditate, or just vibe out to ambient music.

where the bees hang out

The Nectar Lounge

An interactive hang out point of The Hive where people can meet up, charge phones, find lost items, and ask questions. There is also a bulletin board for messages. You can chill, hang out, network.

where the bees eat

The Buzz Boost

A vibrant food haven offering a mouthwatering variety of flavors from local vendors and international cuisines. Whether you're craving something savory, sweet, or a refreshing drink, this is the place to satisfy your hunger and fuel up for the next adventure. With a lively atmosphere, communal seating, and food trucks lining the area, it's where you can refuel, relax, and enjoy Honey's flavors.

where the bees have fun

The Pollination Station

A lively zone where imagination takes the lead and hands get messy. From pottery to digital art, the Pollination Stations offers a variety of workshops where you can learn new skills, express your creativity, and leave with something you’ve made. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned artist, it’s the perfect space to dive into your artistic side and take home a piece of Honey's magic.



your private things

Toilets

Use of the toilets is free of charge at Honey. There are wooden floors in the toilets area, which are cleaned regularly. There are special Honeydrops of Freshness at the festival site that allow you to freshen up.

It is strictly prohibited to urinate in public. When caught urinating in public you will be removed from the festival grounds.



to help you

First aid

During the opening hours of the festival a first aid team of United with Care and a doctor will be permanently present at Tomorrowland.

If you need to bring medication to the festival, please make sure you carry a doctor’s certificate with you to show at the entrance.



phone needs

Charging phone stations

It is possible to charge your phone at both Tomorrowland at one of the Charge your Phone stations. This service is not for free.

Charge your phone so you can reach your friends and share your experience on social media.
